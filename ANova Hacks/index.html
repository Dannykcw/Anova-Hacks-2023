Climate Change

A Perilous Predicament.

Staggering Statistics

  • Global average sea level has risen 8–9 inches (21–24 centimeters) since 1880.

  • High-tide flooding is now 300% to more than 900% more frequent than it was 50 years ago.

  • The rate of global sea level rise is accelerating: It has gone from 0.06 inches (1.4 millimeters) per year to 0.14 inches (3.6 millimeters) per year

  • In 2018, the cost of coastal flooding and erosion caused by rising sea levels in the United States was estimated to be $9.4 billion.

  • The top five nations affected by rising sea levels are China, India, Bangladesh, Vietnam, and Indonesia, with nearly 205 million people at risk.

  • The melting of the Greenland Ice Sheet alone contributed to a sea-level rise of 1.5 millimeters during the period from 2003 to 2013.

  • The number of people exposed to coastal flooding each year could increase from 250 million today to as many as one billion by 2050 due to sea-level rise, coastal erosion, and increased storminess.

  • Scientists predict that all corals will be threatened by 2050 and that 75 percent will face high to critical threat levels.

  • As sea levels rise, low-lying coastal areas where many species live can be permanently or temporarily flooded, resulting in habitat loss for animals like birds, marine mammals, and sea turtles.
